WEBSITES ABOUT PROTECTED AREAS ISSUES

A protected area is a clearly demarcated piece of land that has been acknowledged, set aside, and is effectively maintained by legal or other means in order to promote the long-term conservation of nature along with the ecosystem services and cultural values that go along with it. Different strategies are employed by governments, organizations, and local groups to establish protected places in our society.
As they serve as habitats for a variety of species and uphold ecological equilibrium, protected areas are essential to the preservation of biological diversity. They can cover a wide variety of ecosystems, such as wetlands, woodlands, marine areas, and more. Apart from their ecological importance, protected places frequently support sustainable tourism, scientific research, and environmental education.

The existing network of protected areas does not adequately represent many environments. For instance, the ocean's protected area is less than 4%. Furthermore underrepresented are freshwater environments. Large tracts of their natural habitat are necessary for some species, particularly large animals like bears and cats, to be able to reproduce and locate food. Many protected areas are separated from other regions of natural habitat, and few are large enough to maintain more than a few individuals of these species. In order to remedy this, species movement across protected areas must be facilitated by the construction of corridors. For many species to survive, protected areas must be connected to form networks or systems; nonetheless, this kind of connectedness is still uncommon.Dangers from widespread, either poorly managed or unlawful human activities taking place within protected areas in many parts of the world are closely associated with inadequate management. These include mining, logging, poaching of protected wildlife, and the encroachment of agricultural and human settlements. Outside of protected regions, human activities that contribute to pollution, climate change, and the introduction of invasive species are frequently dangerous.

There are some mechanisms to protect these areas, the most widely used ones are:

  1. Governmental intervention, which can take place locally, regionally, or nationally.
  2. Community-based projects carried out by natives and established organizations.
  3. The acquisition and possession of land by private individuals and institutions.
  4. Areas that were safeguarded by co-management contracts.
  5. Creation of marine laboratories or biological field stations.

Importance of protecting areas

  • To give untamed flora and fauna safe havens.
  • To uphold and enhance Indigenous knowledge and value systems and apply them to the preservation of cultural practices and landscapes that are important to Indigenous peoples and all living things.
  • To make us more resistant to the effects of climate change.
  • To preserve healthy ecosystems and the advantages they offer.
  • To increase knowledge and comprehension of how human activity affects natural systems.
  • To protect important gene pools.
